What is The Best Food For Your Cat?

What makes a Good food?

Your cat’s diet needs to include the right balance of the six major nutrient groups in order maintain optimal health: proteins, fats and oils, minerals, vitamins, carbohydrates and water. Any good quality manufactured cat food should provide your cat with this basic nutritional balance. Many others are designed to meet the specific needs, for example if a cat is pregnant, old or a kitten. As a general rule, the order of ingredients is just as important as the kind of ingredients. Look for foods that list meat (protein) as the first ingredient and have a minimal number of ingredients, indicating low levels of processing. Unless a cat is on a special diet for medical or other reasons, the protein source should be listed first followed by other ingredients in the order of their percentage of total weight.

Conclusion

Overall you should feed your cat a combination of wet and dry food especially if you notice that your cat is not drinking enough water. Wet food provides water that a cat could miss in their normal diet and could help prevent against dehydration. Talk to your vet before switching your cat's food to make sure it would be beneficial for your cat.
